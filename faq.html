About Counselling
Marital counselling is a form of professional therapy designed to help couples identify and resolve conflicts, improve communication, rebuild intimacy, and strengthen their relationship. At Marital Transformative Consult, we integrate biblical wisdom with evidence-based therapeutic techniques to address the spiritual, emotional, and relational dimensions of marriage. Counselling helps couples not just to survive their challenges but to emerge stronger, closer and more aligned.
Faith-based counselling incorporates biblical principles, prayer, and spiritual direction alongside professional therapeutic methods. Rather than treating marriage purely as a human relationship, we view it as a divine covenant, which means we draw on God's Word as the ultimate source of wisdom and healing. That said, our counsellors are fully trained professionals — so you receive both the spiritual and clinical dimensions of support.
For couples counselling and therapy, joint sessions are most effective and preferred. However, we also offer individual sessions where one spouse attends alone — either to prepare for joint counselling, or when a partner is unwilling to participate initially. We have found that often, positive change in one partner creates openness in the other. We also offer individual support as a standalone service for personal healing and growth.

Premarital Counselling
We recommend beginning premarital counselling at least 3–6 months before your wedding date. This gives adequate time to work through all the important topics without rushing. Starting earlier also means that if any significant issues emerge, there is time to address them properly. We have worked with couples as late as one month before their wedding — it is never too late — but earlier is always better.
Our premarital counselling covers: understanding the marriage covenant and its biblical foundation; communication styles and conflict resolution; financial management and compatibility; sexual intimacy and expectations; in-law and extended family dynamics; spiritual life and faith practices as a couple; roles, responsibilities and leadership in marriage; parenting values and plans; and identifying and addressing any pre-existing personal issues that could affect the marriage.
